My work focuses on the intersection of mechanical design, microfluidics, and tissue engineering—specifically developing joint-on-chip systems to model osteoarthritis progression and advance therapeutic testing.

As I approach graduation, I'm focused on gaining industry experience that bridges my research background with real-world medical device development. Most of my technical experience has been research-based, so I'm eager to understand what day-to-day work in industry looks like and discover what truly resonates with me professionally. Projects

Joint-on-Chip Mechanotransduction Platform

August 2024 - Present | Neu Research Lab

Designed and fabricated a microfluidic device to investigate osteoarthritis progression through controlled mechanical stimulation of cartilage and bone tissues. Addressing the $140B annual burden of OA in the US, this platform overcomes limitations of existing models by replicating the complex mechanical environment of human joints—something 2D cultures and animal models cannot achieve.

Key Contributions: Engineered multi-layer PDMS chip architecture in SolidWorks with integrated membrane system. Optimized fabrication protocols including plasma bonding and 50 micron membrane integration. Developed pressure-driven actuation system generating reproducible cyclic shear deformation. Performed confocal microscopy imaging and ImageJ analysis across 12 experimental trials, achieving less than 15% device-to-device variance. Currently building ABAQUS finite element model to predict tissue-level stress distributions under physiological loading.

SolidWorks ABAQUS FEA PDMS Fabrication Microfluidics Confocal Microscopy ImageJ Tissue Engineering
