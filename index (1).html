Joint-on-Chip Mechanotransduction Platform

August 2024 – Present

Designed and fabricated a microfluidic device to investigate osteoarthritis progression through controlled mechanical stimulation of cartilage and bone tissues. Current experimental models fail to replicate the physiological mechanical environment that drives joint disease, limiting drug development and treatment strategies. This platform addresses that gap by integrating pressure-driven actuation with multi-compartment architecture to generate shear forces that mimic natural joint articulation.

Key Contributions

  • Designed multi-layer chip architecture in SolidWorks featuring separate shear and compression chambers with integrated membrane interface
  • Optimized PDMS fabrication workflow including plasma bonding protocols and 50 μm membrane integration to prevent air leakage
  • Developed controlled actuation system using synchronized syringe pumps to generate reproducible cyclic shear deformation
  • Performed confocal microscopy imaging and ImageJ displacement analysis across 12 trials, achieving <15% device-to-device variance
  • Building ABAQUS finite element model to predict tissue-level stress distributions under physiological loading conditions
  • Establishing sterile workflows for sequential bone and cartilage tissue integration and viability assessment protocols

This platform addresses critical limitations in osteoarthritis research by providing human-relevant mechanical stimuli that conventional models cannot replicate. By enabling controlled investigation of cartilage-bone mechanotransduction, the device supports future studies on early disease mechanisms and therapeutic interventions—advancing toward personalized treatment strategies for the 88% of adults over 45 affected by OA.

Biomimetic Brain Phantom for Quantitative MRI

August 2024 – Present • 6-Person Team • CaliberMRI Partnership

Served as Test & Systems Engineer on a cross-functional team developing a quantitative MRI brain phantom in partnership with CaliberMRI to standardize clinical imaging calibration. Designed and executed comprehensive verification testing program to validate phantom performance against stakeholder specifications. Developed three critical test protocols: spectrophotometric diffusion analysis, mechanical compression testing, and bubble-leak sealing evaluation.

Key Contributions

  • Designed spectrophotometric diffusion test protocol quantifying solute transfer across 300 µm barriers over 48 hours
  • Developed mechanical compression testing procedure validating structural integrity under assembly loads with safety margins
  • Created bubble-leak evaluation protocol with vacuum and pressure testing to assess seal performance
  • Conducted diffusion experiments demonstrating <1% solute transfer, confirming effective compartment isolation
  • Performed mechanical testing validating walls withstand required loads with adequate safety margin
  • Collaborated with CaliberMRI stakeholders to align test protocols with clinical imaging requirements
  • Analyzed multi-modal experimental data and uncertainty propagation to validate design specifications

Through rigorous experimental validation, confirmed the phantom system meets critical performance specifications for compartment isolation, structural integrity, and seal reliability. The comprehensive testing program established manufacturing feasibility and identified integration requirements necessary for advancing toward barrier-free phantom designs, enabling CaliberMRI to pursue clinical standardization of quantitative MRI measurements across healthcare institutions.
